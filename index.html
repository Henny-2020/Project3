Decolonizing Artistic Hiring Practices

A conference for the advancement of inclusive, anti-racist, equal, and diverse hiring processes within the arts sector. For Employers, Businesses, and Organisations seeking to make a difference in their community.

120 W North Ave #302, Baltimore, MD 21201 November 20, 2020

Panelists

Michael J. Bobbit

Michael J. Bobbitt is the Artistic Director of New Repertory Theatre in Boston, MA. He is an arts leader, director, choreographer, playwright and Anti-racist Arts Advocate.

Talk # 1: Building Anti-Racist Organisations - 11:00 A.M.

Michael J. Bobbitt discusses the problem of racism in the workplace. He shares how organasations can conduct anti-racist, equal, and diverse hiring practices.

Emily Elder Cory

Emily works with Single Carrot Theatre as their Executive Director! Single Carrot is a professional theatre company in Baltimore City that creates socially relevant theatre in traditional and non-traditional spaces.

Talk # 2: Elevated, Equitable, and Accessible Arts Education - 1:00 P.M.

Emily shares how companies can execute programs, professional development, resources, and events that further improve equality within the arts and education sector.

Suzan E. Jenkins

Suzan E. Jenkins is a leader in the non-profit arts and culture sector having served in executive positions at the Rhythm and Blues Foundation, the Smithsonian Institution and the Recording Industry Association of America.

Talk # 3: Diversity in Positions of Leadership - 3:00 P.M.

Suzan E. Jenkins discusses leadership and shares a wealth of experience as a distinguished entrepreneur and woman of color in the non-profit arts and culture sector
